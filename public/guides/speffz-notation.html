Mastering Speffz Notation

Complete guide to edge and corner notation for blindfold cubing

What is Speffz Notation?

Speffz notation is a lettering system that assigns a unique letter to each piece position on a Rubik's cube. This system is essential for blindfold cubing because it allows you to encode the cube's state as a sequence of letters, which can then be memorized using letter pairs.

Why Speffz Notation Matters:

  • Each edge piece position has a unique letter (A-X)
  • Each corner piece position has a unique letter (A-X)
  • Letters represent positions, not pieces themselves
  • Understanding notation is the foundation of BLD memorization

Edge Notation System

Edge pieces are assigned letters based on their position on the cube. There are 12 edge positions, each with a unique letter in the Speffz system.

How Edge Notation Works:
  • Each edge position has two stickers (colors)
  • The letter represents the position, not the piece
  • You must recognize which letter corresponds to each position
  • Orientation matters - an edge can be flipped

Corner Notation System

Corner pieces are assigned letters based on their position on the cube. There are 8 corner positions, each with a unique letter in the Speffz system.

How Corner Notation Works:
  • Each corner position has three stickers (colors)
  • The letter represents the position, not the piece
  • You must recognize which letter corresponds to each position
  • Orientation matters - a corner can be twisted in three ways

Edge Tracing Practice

Edge tracing is the process of following how edge pieces move through cycles during a scramble. This is a fundamental skill for BLD edge memorization.

What is Edge Tracing:
  • Start with a scrambled cube
  • Follow how edge pieces move through cycles
  • Convert the cycle to a sequence of Speffz letters
  • This sequence is what you memorize for edges
Practice with Edge Tracing Drill:
Our Edge Tracing Drill helps you practice:
  • Tracing edge cycles from scramble strings
  • Inputting the correct letter sequence
  • Understanding how edges move through cycles
  • Building the foundation for edge memorization
Edge Tracing Tips:
  • Start with simple scrambles and work up to complex ones
  • Trace one cycle at a time
  • Verify your tracing by checking the cycle closes
  • Practice until tracing becomes automatic

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Here are common mistakes beginners make with Speffz notation and how to avoid them:

Mistake 1: Confusing Positions and Pieces
Problem: Thinking the letter represents the piece itself, not the position.
Solution: Remember that letters represent positions. The same piece can be in different positions with different letters.
Mistake 2: Ignoring Orientation
Problem: Not considering that edges can be flipped and corners can be twisted.
Solution: Learn how orientation affects notation and practice recognizing oriented vs. misoriented pieces.
Mistake 3: Not Practicing Regularly
Problem: Learning the scheme but not practicing recognition until it's automatic.
Solution: Practice notation drills daily until recognition is instant and automatic.
Mistake 4: Rushing Through Drills
Problem: Prioritizing speed over accuracy in notation recognition.
Solution: Focus on accuracy first. Speed will come naturally with practice.
