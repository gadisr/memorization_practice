🧠 How to Solve a Rubik's Cube Blindfolded

A comprehensive beginner's guide to blindfolded cubing

🎯 What is Blindfolded Cubing?

Blindfolded cubing (often called "BLD" for "Blindfolded") is the advanced technique of solving a Rubik's cube without looking at it during the solving process. It's one of the most impressive and challenging disciplines in speedcubing.

🧠 The Mental Challenge:

Think of it like memorizing a complex sequence of moves for a dance routine, then performing it perfectly without being able to see your feet. You must rely entirely on your memory and spatial awareness to execute the solution correctly.

🔧 The Process (How It Works)

1 Inspection Phase 👀
You have 15 minutes to study the scrambled cube and memorize the positions of all pieces. This is where you create your mental map of what needs to be moved where.
2 Blindfold Application 🎭
Once you've memorized the cube state, you put on the blindfold. From this point forward, you cannot see the cube and must rely entirely on your memory.
3 Execution Phase ⚡
You execute the memorized sequence of moves to solve the cube. This typically involves solving corners first, then edges, using specific algorithms for each piece.

🧩 The Memory System: Letter Pairs

The key to blindfolded cubing is an efficient memory system. Most cubers use letter pairs combined with visual imagery to encode and recall piece positions.

🧠 Memory Palace Technique:

Instead of trying to remember raw positions like "corner A → position B," cubers create memorable associations:

  • AB might become "Apple Banana" 🍎🍌
  • CD might become "Cat Dog" 🐱🐶
  • EF might become "Elephant Fish" 🐘🐠

These pairs are then linked into a story or journey: "An Apple and Banana are playing with a Cat and Dog, while an Elephant and Fish watch!" The more vivid and memorable the imagery, the easier it is to recall during execution.

🎓 Learning Progression

1 Master Regular Solving 🧩
You need to be proficient at solving a Rubik's cube sighted first. This means being comfortable with basic algorithms and understanding cube mechanics.
2 Learn Letter Notation 🔤
Study the Speffz lettering system to understand how each piece position is assigned a letter. This is your foundation for creating letter pairs.
3 Practice Memory Techniques 🧠
Start with 2-3 letter pairs and develop your own imagery system. Practice until you can consistently recall these pairs.
4 Build Up Complexity 📈
Gradually increase to 5-10 pairs, then work toward the full 20+ pairs needed for a complete solve.
5 Execute Your First BLD Solve 🏆
Once you can memorize and recall 20+ letter pairs, attempt your first full blindfolded solve!
